Espresso
The Heart of Coffee
Espresso is the foundation of countless coffee drinks — from cappuccinos to lattes. It’s made by forcing hot water through finely ground coffee under high pressure, producing a small, concentrated shot with a signature crema on top.
How It’s Brewed:
Finely grind fresh coffee beans (espresso grind).
Tamp the grounds firmly into the portafilter.
Extract 30ml of espresso under 9 bars of pressure for about 25–30 seconds.
Perfect For: Cappuccino, Latte, Americano, Macchiato.
Pro Tip: If the shot tastes sour — grind finer. If it tastes bitter — grind coarser. Balance is the secret.
French Press
Rustic, Full-Bodied, Classic
The French Press, also called a Cafetière, is one of the simplest yet most satisfying brewing methods. It uses immersion rather than filtration, allowing natural oils and flavors to shine.
How It’s Brewed:
Coarsely grind the beans.
Add 1 part coffee to 15 parts hot water (~200°F).
Stir gently, then steep for 4 minutes.
Press the plunger down slowly and pour immediately.
Perfect For: Strong morning brews and unfiltered flavor lovers.
Pro Tip:
Don’t let the coffee sit too long after plunging — it’ll over-extract and taste bitter.
Pour over
Art in Every Drop
The Pour Over brewing is about patience and precision. The method highlights the delicate flavors of high-quality beans by controlling the flow and temperature of water.
How It’s Brewed:
Use a medium grind coffee.
Place filter and rinse it with hot water.
Add grounds and pour water slowly in circles, allowing blooming.
Continue pouring gradually until total brew time reaches 3–4 minutes.
Perfect For: Light to medium roast lovers who enjoy clarity and aroma.
Pro Tip:
Control your pour — too fast and it’s weak, too slow and it’s bitter. Steady rhythm = perfection.
Cold Brew
Patience in a Cup
Cold Brew is all about time — not temperature. Instead of heat, it uses long steeping (12–18 hours) in cold water to extract smooth, naturally sweet flavors.
How It’s Brewed:
Use coarsely ground beans.
Mix 1 part coffee with 5 parts cold water.
Steep in the fridge for 12–18 hours.
Strain and serve over ice or with milk.
Perfect For: Hot days and cold coffee lovers.
Pro Tip:
Use filtered water — cold brew highlights purity. And don’t rush it!
AeroPress
Modern Innovation, Timeless Taste
The AeroPress is a newer invention but has already become a cult favorite. It combines air pressure and immersion for a quick, rich brew that’s versatile and easy to clean.

Each brew is a story — slow, rich, and deeply human. Find the one that speaks to your rhythm.
